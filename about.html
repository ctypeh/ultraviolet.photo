35mm half-frame diptych of ClaireViolet in the Seattle Central Library by mb bischoff.

ClaireViolet (it/its) is a transsexual 35mm film photographer from Capitol Hill, Seattle. Its panoramic work explores depersonalization and the mechanical. Its favorite color is yellow.

Exhibitions & Publications

  • ClaireViolet's photographic work was been exhibited in a solo gallery show at Elevation Project (Seattle) in January 2026, featuring a broad overview of its work.
  • Its work will also be part of Hearth and Home, at 3rd Story Gallery (Brooklyn, NY) a curated exhibition for Trans Art Fest NYC from May 16th through June 2026.
  • It has also been published in the second edition of Easel, a “print-first photographic journal made by transsexuals.”

Current Projects

It is currently working on two projects, both primarily in 35mm panoramas. The first series, Maritime, Manufacturing, and Logistics focuses on logistics infrastructure and industrial land use along the urban west coast, especially Seattle, Washington and Oakland, California. The second is a collection of North American transit photography which it began in April 2025. This series currently features work from New York City, the San Francisco Bay Area, and Seattle. Its desire is “to depict the feeling of awe I experience at scale, and my appreciation for the organized chaos that underlies our world.”
