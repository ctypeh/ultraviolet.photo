Maritime, Manufacturing, and Logistics

Preview this project on Glass

“Maritime Manufacturing and Logistics (MML) Zoning ... An existing industrial area with a concentration of core industrial and maritime uses including manufacturing, warehousing, shipping, and logistics activities and is well served with truck, rail, and maritime or freight infrastructure.”

from a City of Seattle report
March 2023

Maritime, Manufacturing, and Logistics (MML) is a zoning code in Seattle that I came across while doing some research earlier this year, which restricts land usage to these three industrial focuses. I started to become curious about this earlier this year as I’ve been spending an increasing amount of time in the SODO and Georgetown “neighborhoods” of the city where this zoning is especially noticable. As I started paying more attention, the shapes and colors of the harbor began to interest me, and this quickly became a captivating personal project, even as I travelled outside of Seattle to other cities on the west coast of North America.

I think it’s particularly well suited to photography with the X-Pan, since the scale of ports and industry here tends toward the wide rather than the tall. Also, it makes everything look like HEAT (1995, dir. Michael Mann) a film that I deeply love.

I've since decided to expand this project to cover the San Francisco Bay Area as well.

North American Transit

Preview this project on Glass

Since October 2024, I have been working on a collection of panoramic color photographs featuring scenes from public transit, which features my favorite metro systems throughout North America, with a focus on New York and the San Francisco Bay Area. I now have over 900 images of public transit from the Bay Area, including BART, which as much as possible feature inviting bright colors and highlight the sense of community commuters and transit riders experience.

People develop a strong sense of ownership toward their local transit systems, and my photographs aim to capture a love for what moves people, literally and figuratively.
