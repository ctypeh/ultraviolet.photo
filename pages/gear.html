35mm Film Cameras and Lenses

Panoramic

  • Hasselblad X-Pan (First Generation) §
    About This unique camera, manufactured by Fujifilm, can take 20 panoramic 65:24 (approx. 2.7:1) images and 36 standard 4:3 aspect-ratio frames on a roll of 35mm film. By unspooling the entire roll of film when first loaded, it is able to measure the length of film and dynamically switch between panoramic and standard frame sizes without wasting any film, thanks to the help of a film masking window, and the unspooling mechanism which re-centers the frame on the lens as the camera switches between modes.

    • PC-NIKKOR 35mm 𝑓2.8 with metal screw-in lens cap. §
      About This architectural shift-lens uses a screw mechanism and a manual aperture to adjust the film plane to be parallel for large subjects where framing may otherwise be difficult without warping the perspective. Because it needs to fully cover a standard 4:3 frame even when shifted to the maximum extent, the image circle is much wider than a standard lens for a 35mm camera, and it can be adapted to the X-Pan's panoramic format with some downsides, including a falloff toward the edges of the frame, and vignetting on the left and right edges of the image¹. The vignetting can be reduced by a slight crop of the final image.
